YouTube parental controls: the right setup for every age

YouTube has three different safety systems. Picking the right one matters more than any single setting.

The three options at a glance

OptionBest forWhat it does
YouTube Kids (separate app) Under ~9 Walled garden of kid content. Age bands (Preschool / Younger / Older), timer, search on/off, or approve-every-video mode.
Supervised experience (main YouTube + Family Link) ~9–13 Regular YouTube with a content level you choose: Explore (9+), Explore More (13+), or Most of YouTube. Comments and uploads restricted.
Restricted Mode Teens / shared devices A coarse filter on the normal app. Easily turned off unless locked via a parental control app or network filter.

Setup: YouTube Kids (under 9)

  1. Install the YouTube Kids app and sign in with your Google account.
  2. Create a profile for your child with their real age — the age band drives what they see.
  3. Choose content settings. For maximum control, pick "Approved content only": your kid can only watch videos, channels, or collections you approve by hand.
  4. Turn search off for younger kids — it shrinks the discovery surface dramatically.
  5. Set the timer (up to 60 min) so the app locks itself when time is up.

Setup: supervised experience (9–13)

  1. Create a child Google account with Family Link (or link their existing one): families.google.com.
  2. In Family Link, open your child → Controls → YouTube.
  3. Pick a content level: "Explore" (roughly 9+), "Explore More" (13+), or "Most of YouTube" (everything except age-restricted).
  4. Review the defaults: autoplay is off, comments are hidden, and your kid can't upload or live-stream. Leave it that way.
Why Restricted Mode alone fails: it's a per-browser, per-device toggle that any kid can switch off in two taps unless something locks it. If your teen uses regular YouTube, lock Restricted Mode through Family Link, your router/DNS, or a parental control app — not the YouTube app itself.

The honest limits

Even with perfect settings, YouTube's recommendation engine occasionally surfaces borderline content, and Shorts is harder to filter than regular videos. The settings above cut 95% of the problem; the rest is co-viewing and conversation.
